NinjaFlex 3D Printing Filament

Open your world to flexible prints with low-tack, easy-to-feed NinjaFlex filaments. NinjaFlex creates extremely flexible, strong prints, extending the capabilities of many of today's best-selling 3D printers. NinjaFlex is a specially formulated thermoplastic elastomer (TPE), ideal for 3D printers with direct-drive extruders. All NinjaFlex filaments are made in and shipped from the U.S. International orders are stocked and shipped from the UK. NinjaFlex is patent pending technology.

Announcing the newest addition to the NinjaFlex family, SemiFlex 3D Filament! Just like the original NinjaFlex 3D Filament, SemiFlex material boasts flexibility, strength and reliability for your 3D printing projects and is slightly more rigid to expand your printing possibilities.
SemiFlex is best for the following types of projects:
  • High level of detail
  • Contain intricate parts
  • High resolution text
  • Unsupported vertical printing
  • Shock-absorption needed
  • Requires less flexibility than NinjaFlex original 3D Filament
To determine which NinjaFlex 3D Filament is best for your next project, use the quick reference chart below.

 
Features:
  • Consistent diameter and material properties provide reliable, high quality prints
  • Patent pending technology allows for smooth feeding
  • Low friction exterior allows smooth feed through filament guides
  • High elasticity and excellent abrasion resistance
  • Excellent build platform adhesion and bonding between layers
  • Filament hardness of approximately 85A (NinjaFlex) and 98A(50D) SemiFlex
  • REACH and RoHS 2002/95/EC Directive Compliant
  • 1.75mm filament spool = .50 kg
  • 3.00mm filament spool = .75 kg
Processing Guidelines:
  • Recommended extruder temperature: 220 - 230C
  • Recommended platform temperature: 20 - 50C
  • Recommended Print Speed: 30 mm/s
Shipments to Europe and the Middle East are made from our United Kingdom facility. All other shipments originate in the United States.

  2. Tricky at first

    Reviewed on Wednesday, April 01, 2015
    100 out of 100

    • Rating

      5 Star
    Review

    This material was tricky to use at first, but the big important things: -Garolite printing platform is a MUST for good adhesion. I sanded mine with 200 grit sandpaper and clean between every use with ISO. Some garolite boards aren't flat, so watch out... -220C extruder temp. works great for me. -Use the ooze control feature in your printing software! And really use the retraction and wipe features too. -Slow down! -If your extruder has any gap between the drive gear and the hot-end input, this stuff will find a way to bend into that gap. I printed a nylon block to bridge that gap on my Bukobot's Spitfire nozzle. After figuring out these tricks, I'm printing GORGEOUS rubber parts. So strong I can't rip, tear or break them - even along the print layers.
